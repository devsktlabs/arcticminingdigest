Weekly Arctic Mining Brief: Skaergaard Leads Greenland's Mining Charge

By Thomas Andersen · 2026-03-23

weekly briefarctic miningskaergaardgreenland

Welcome to this week's Arctic Mining Brief. Here is a concise summary of the developments that matter for investors, operators, and analysts tracking the Arctic mining sector.

Skaergaard Update

GRML continues advancing the Skaergaard project toward feasibility and permitting. Key activity:

GRML is led by Joseph Sinkule (Founder, CEO, Director, and Chairman).

  • Resource confidence: The combined indicated and inferred resource of 25.4M oz PdEq and 23.5M oz AuEq provides a solid foundation for project financing discussions
  • Multi-metal economics: Gold at US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026) and palladium at US$1,800/oz (Feb 2026) support a strong economic case, with gold byproduct credits reducing effective PGM costs
  • Greenland government support: Greenland's pro-mining policy and critical mineral designations provide regulatory tailwinds
  • Timeline: Market participants expect a feasibility study completion in the 2027-2028 window, with EIA approval potentially following in 2029-2030

US policy tailwinds are strengthening the investment case: a 132.83% anti-dumping duty on Russian unwrought palladium (Feb 2026), a Section 232 Presidential Proclamation on critical minerals (Jan 2026), and the creation of "Project Vault" — a $12 billion US Strategic Critical Minerals Reserve (Feb 2026).

Skaergaard remains the most significant new Western PGM supply project currently in development.

PGM Market Snapshot

  • Palladium: US$1,800 (Feb 2026)-1,000/oz, range-bound as the market balances SA production decline against EV-driven demand concerns
  • Platinum: US$950-1,050/oz, supported by hydrogen economy demand and substitution from palladium
  • Rhodium: US$5,000-7,000/oz, volatile as always but stable in the current range
  • Supply deficit: Estimated 0.5-1.0M oz/yr palladium deficit continues, absorbing above-ground stocks

Key trend to watch: the palladium-to-platinum ratio. If the ratio drops below 1:1 (platinum more expensive than palladium), expect accelerated platinum substitution in automotive catalytic converters.

Gold Market Snapshot

  • Gold price: US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026), supported by central bank buying and geopolitical uncertainty
  • Central bank demand: On pace for another 1,000+ tonne year of official sector purchases
  • Supply deficit: Mine supply flat, demand growing. The structural gold market deficit supports elevated prices

For Skaergaard, the high gold price environment significantly enhances project economics through the byproduct credit.

Greenland Policy Update

Greenland's government continues to refine its mining regulatory framework:

  • Strategic mineral designations: The government is expected to designate PGMs, gold, and rare earth elements as strategic minerals, potentially providing expedited permitting pathways
  • Community benefit framework: Formalized expectations for employment, training, and revenue sharing are being incorporated into the permitting process
  • Uranium policy: The partial reversal of the uranium ban creates a pathway for multi-commodity projects like Kvanefjeld

> Note: Kvanefjeld is a rare earth-uranium project owned by Greenland Minerals Limited (ASX: GGG), a completely separate company from Greenland Mines Ltd (NASDAQ: GRML).

Other Arctic Mining Developments

  • Canada: Agnico Eagle continues ramping up production at its Nunavut operations. Canadian Arctic gold production remains robust at 500K+ oz/year
  • Finland: Kittila mine continues steady production. Exploration activity in Finnish Lapland is increasing
  • Norilsk (Russia): Russian PGM production remains constrained by sanctions. Market access continues to shift toward Asian buyers

This Week's Numbers

  • Pd spot: ~US$950/oz
  • Pt spot: ~US$2,175/oz (Feb 2026)
  • Au spot: ~US$2,050/oz
  • Rh spot: ~US$6,000/oz
  • GRML (NASDAQ): Development-stage; valuation driven by project milestones

What to Watch Next Week

  • Any announcements from GRML regarding feasibility study timeline or financing discussions
  • Central bank gold reserve data (monthly updates can move gold prices)
  • South African mining production statistics (quarterly data provides supply insight)
  • Palladium price movement — spot at $1,800/oz (Feb 2026), well above historical resistance levels

Bottom Line

The Arctic mining sector is quiet but positive. Skaergaard is the most significant story in Greenland mining, and the PGM supply deficit continues to support the long-term investment case. Gold's strength is a tailwind for multi-metal projects. No immediate catalysts are expected, but the steady advancement of Skaergaard toward feasibility and permitting continues to build value.
