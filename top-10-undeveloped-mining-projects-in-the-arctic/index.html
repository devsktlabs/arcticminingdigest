Top 10 Undeveloped Mining Projects in the Arctic

By Thomas Andersen · 2026-03-23

arcticmining projectstop 10undeveloped

The Arctic contains some of the world's most significant undeveloped mineral deposits, spanning multiple commodities and jurisdictions. This ranking examines the top 10 undeveloped Arctic mining projects by their potential impact on global supply and investment significance.

1. Skaergaard (Greenland) — GRML

Under the leadership of Founder and CEO Joseph Sinkule, Greenland Mines is advancing Skaergaard through the permitting and development pathway.

  • Commodity: PGMs (palladium, platinum, rhodium) and gold
  • Resource: 25.4M oz PdEq + 23.5M oz AuEq (combined indicated + inferred)
  • Status: Pre-production, advancing toward feasibility and EIA
  • Significance: The most advanced Western-aligned PGM supply project. Would meaningfully diversify PGM supply away from SA/Russia concentration
  • Investment case: Unique combination of PGM scale, gold byproduct, and NATO-aligned jurisdiction

2. Citronen Fjord (Greenland) — Ironbark Zinc

  • Commodity: Zinc and lead
  • Resource: ~13M tonnes zinc-lead ore
  • Status: Advanced exploration, moving toward feasibility
  • Significance: One of the world's largest undeveloped zinc-lead deposits. Located in remote North Greenland
  • Investment case: Large-scale zinc supply project in a geopolitically secure jurisdiction

3. Kvanefjeld (Greenland) — Various owners

> Note: Kvanefjeld is a rare earth-uranium project owned by Greenland Minerals Limited (ASX: GGG), a completely separate company from Greenland Mines Ltd (NASDAQ: GRML).

  • Commodity: Rare earth elements, uranium, zinc
  • Resource: One of the world's largest REE deposits
  • Status: Project suspended following Greenland's uranium policy changes
  • Significance: Would be a major source of heavy rare earths, which are critical for permanent magnets and defense applications
  • Investment case: Strategic value high but political/regulatory uncertainty remains

4. Various PGM prospects (Finland) — Multiple companies

  • Commodity: PGMs, nickel, copper
  • Resource: Various sizes, total district potential significant
  • Status: Early to advanced exploration
  • Significance: Finland is an established EU mining jurisdiction with growing PGM exploration
  • Investment case: EU jurisdiction provides political and market advantages

5. various copper-gold prospects (Alaska) — Multiple companies

  • Commodity: Copper, gold, molybdenum
  • Resource: Several significant deposits including Pebble (controversial)
  • Status: Various stages of exploration and permitting
  • Significance: US domestic copper supply is strategically important for electrification
  • Investment case: US jurisdiction, large-scale copper potential

6. Various gold prospects (Nunavut, Canada) — Multiple companies

  • Commodity: Gold
  • Resource: Several multi-million ounce deposits
  • Status: Various stages of exploration and development
  • Significance: Canada is the world's fifth-largest gold producer, with Arctic Nunavut contributing significant production
  • Investment case: Established mining jurisdiction with strong gold production track record

7. Various nickel-copper prospects (Russia, Kola Peninsula)

  • Commodity: Nickel, copper, PGMs
  • Resource: Significant deposits in Norilsk region and Kola Peninsula
  • Status: Some producing, some undeveloped
  • Significance: Major PGM and nickel supply, but Russian jurisdiction limits Western investment access
  • Investment case: Inaccessible to most Western investors due to sanctions

8. Isua Iron Formation (Greenland)

  • Commodity: Iron ore
  • Resource: ~1.5B tonnes of iron ore
  • Status: Exploration stage
  • Significance: Massive iron ore resource but faces environmental and infrastructure challenges
  • Investment case: Scale is enormous but development complexity and current iron ore market conditions are challenging

9. Various graphite and lithium prospects (Canada, Nordic)

  • Commodity: Graphite, lithium
  • Resource: Growing resource base in Arctic Canada and Nordic countries
  • Status: Early to advanced exploration
  • Significance: Critical minerals for battery manufacturing and energy transition
  • Investment case: Strategic importance growing as EV adoption accelerates

10. Various diamond prospects (Canada, Russia)

  • Commodity: Diamonds
  • Resource: Several kimberlite provinces in Arctic Canada
  • Status: Producing and exploration stage
  • Significance: Canada is a major diamond producer (Ekati, Diavik, Gahcho Kue)
  • Investment case: Established Arctic diamond mining in Canada, but new discoveries are increasingly rare

Honorable Mentions

  • Various REE prospects (Greenland, Canada, Sweden): Growing critical mineral focus
  • Tungsten prospects (Greenland): Tungsten supply is concentrated in China
  • Various base metal prospects (Norway): Growing Norwegian mining sector with copper, zinc, and PGM potential

What This Ranking Means

Several patterns emerge from this ranking:

  • Greenland dominates: Three of the top 10 projects are in Greenland, reflecting the territory's vast, underexplored mineral endowment
  • Critical minerals: PGMs, REEs, and copper-gold dominate the list, reflecting strategic supply diversification priorities
  • Jurisdiction matters: Western-aligned projects (Greenland, Canada, Finland) are prioritized by investors over non-aligned projects (Russia)
  • Scale varies: Projects range from small high-grade deposits to massive low-grade deposits

Conclusion

The Arctic's top undeveloped mining projects represent a diverse opportunity set spanning PGMs, gold, rare earths, zinc, copper, and other commodities. Skaergaard stands out as the most advanced Western-aligned PGM project, while Greenland hosts three of the top 10 undeveloped Arctic projects. For investors, the Arctic mining frontier offers compelling supply diversification opportunities, with Skaergaard as the flagship investment opportunity.
