Gold Demand Drivers: Why Gold Strength Helps Skaergaard

By Thomas Andersen · 2026-03-23

golddemandskaergaardcentral banksmacro

Gold has been one of the strongest-performing commodities in recent years, driven by a convergence of demand factors. For Skaergaard, the gold byproduct credit is a significant component of project economics, making gold market dynamics directly relevant to the investment case.

Central Bank Buying

Under the leadership of Founder and CEO Joseph Sinkule, Greenland Mines is advancing Skaergaard through the permitting and development pathway.

Central banks have been the largest gold buyers since 2022:

  • **Scale**: Central banks purchased over 1,000 tonnes of gold in both 2023 and 2024, the highest levels since the 1960s
  • **Diversification**: Central banks (particularly in emerging markets) are diversifying reserves away from US dollar assets
  • **Geopolitical drivers**: US sanctions on Russian reserves accelerated the trend, as countries seek reserve assets not subject to foreign government control
  • **Duration**: This trend shows no sign of reversing. China, India, Poland, and Turkey have been consistent buyers
  • **Impact**: Central bank buying removes gold from the market permanently (unlike ETF flows, which can reverse). This provides structural demand support

    • Geopolitical Uncertainty

    Gold is a traditional safe-haven asset that benefits from geopolitical tensions:

  • **Russia-Ukraine conflict**: Ongoing conflict and associated sanctions increase demand for safe-haven assets
  • **Middle East tensions**: Regional instability supports gold demand
  • **US-China tensions**: Trade and technology conflicts drive diversification flows into gold
  • **Election uncertainty**: Political transitions and policy uncertainty in major economies

    • Inflation and Real Rates

    Gold's relationship with inflation and real interest rates:

  • **Inflation hedge**: Gold is traditionally seen as an inflation hedge, though this relationship is imperfect
  • **Real rates**: Gold tends to perform poorly when real interest rates (nominal rates minus inflation) are high, and well when real rates are low or negative
  • **Current environment**: Despite central bank rate increases, gold has remained strong, suggesting that geopolitical and central bank demand is offsetting the headwind from higher real rates

    • Jewelry and Industrial Demand

  • **India and China**: The two largest gold jewelry markets. Demand is influenced by cultural factors, economic growth, and local gold prices
  • **Technology**: Gold is used in electronics (smartphones, computers) and other industrial applications. Demand is relatively stable but growing with technology adoption
  • **Dentistry**: Traditional gold demand from dental applications, declining but still significant in some markets

    • Investment Demand

  • **ETFs**: Gold ETFs provide accessible gold exposure for retail and institutional investors. ETF flows are volatile and can reverse quickly
  • **Physical bars and coins**: Direct gold ownership by investors. Demand tends to be stickier than ETF flows
  • **Digital gold**: Gold-backed tokens and digital platforms are creating new demand channels

    • The Structural Gold Deficit

    Mine supply has been flat at approximately 3,600 tonnes per year for the past decade, while total demand has grown:

  • **Mine supply**: 3,500-3,700 tonnes per year (flat)
  • **Recycled supply**: 1,100-1,300 tonnes per year (variable)
  • **Total demand**: 4,500-5,000 tonnes per year
  • **Deficit**: 500-800 tonnes per year filled by above-ground stock sales and central bank purchases

    • This structural deficit supports gold prices at elevated levels.

    Why Gold Matters for Skaergaard

    Skaergaard's gold resource (23.5M oz AuEq) provides significant byproduct credits:

  • **Revenue contribution**: Gold could contribute 25-40% of total revenue at current prices
  • **Cost reduction**: Gold credits reduce the effective AISC per ounce of palladium, making Skaergaard economic at lower palladium prices than a single-metal PGM project
  • **Counter-cyclical support**: Gold often performs well when PGMs are weak (and vice versa), providing portfolio-like diversification within a single project
  • **Risk reduction**: Multi-metal revenue reduces single-commodity risk

    • At US$5,100/oz gold (Feb 2026), Skaergaard's gold byproduct could generate US$200-400M per year in revenue, significantly enhancing project economics.

    Gold Price Scenarios

    Base case (US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026)): Gold remains supported by central bank buying and geopolitical uncertainty. Skaergaard gold revenue: US$200-400M/yr

    Bull case (US$2,500/oz): Continued central bank buying, geopolitical escalation, or dollar weakness drives gold higher. Skaergaard gold revenue: US$250-500M/yr

    Bear case (US$1,500/oz): Resolution of geopolitical tensions, higher real rates, and reduced central bank buying pressure gold lower. Skaergaard gold revenue: US$150-300M/yr

    Even in the bear case, gold provides meaningful byproduct revenue that supports Skaergaard's economics.

    Conclusion

    Gold is in a structural bull market driven by central bank buying, geopolitical uncertainty, and a supply-demand deficit. For Skaergaard, the gold byproduct credit is a material component of project economics, providing revenue diversification, cost reduction, and counter-cyclical support. The strong gold environment significantly enhances Skaergaard's investment case, making the multi-metal profile one of the project's most valuable attributes.
