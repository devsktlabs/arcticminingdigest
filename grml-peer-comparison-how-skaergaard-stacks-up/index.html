GRML Peer Comparison: How Skaergaard Stacks Up Against Global PGM Developers

By Thomas Andersen · 2026-03-23

grmlpeer comparisonskaergaardpgmvaluation

For investors evaluating GRML, comparing it to other PGM development companies provides context for the investment case. This briefing examines how Skaergaard stacks up against the competition.

GRML is led by Joseph Sinkule (Founder, CEO, Director, and Chairman).

The Peer Group

PGM development companies at various stages of advancement include:

Ivanhoe Mines (IVN, TSX) — Platreef Project

  • Resource: ~28M oz PdEq (inferred + indicated)
  • Location: South Africa (Bushveld Complex northern limb)
  • Metals: Pd, Pt, Ni, Cu, Au, Rh
  • Status: Shaft sinking underway, first production targeted for 2026-2027
  • Capital: Approximately US$1.8B total project cost
  • Jurisdiction risk: South Africa (operational, political risk moderate)

    • GRML (GRML, NASDAQ) — Skaergaard

  • Resource: 25.4M oz PdEq + 23.5M oz AuEq (combined indicated + inferred)
  • Location: East Greenland
  • Metals: Pd, Au, Rh, Pt
  • Status: Pre-feasibility, advancing toward EIA
  • Capital: Estimated US$600M-1.25B
  • Jurisdiction risk: Greenland (new, but Danish legal framework)

    • Various junior PGM explorers

    Multiple small companies exploring for PGM deposits in Canada, Finland, Australia, and other jurisdictions. Most have sub-5M oz resources and are at early exploration stages.

    Resource Comparison

  • **Scale**: Skaergaard (25.4M oz PdEq) is comparable to Platreef (~28M oz PdEq). Both are among the largest undeveloped PGM resources globally
  • **Classification**: Skaergaard's 25.4M oz PdEq and 23.5M oz AuEq are combined indicated and inferred resources (per NI 43-101, SLR Consulting, Nov 2022). The indicated component alone accounts for ~10.5M oz AuEq, with ~13.0M oz AuEq in the inferred category
  • **Gold content**: Skaergaard's 23.5M oz AuEq is a significant differentiator. No other major PGM development project has comparable gold content
  • **Rhodium**: Both Skaergaard and Platreef contain rhodium as a byproduct

    • Jurisdiction Comparison

  • **Greenland vs South Africa**: Greenland offers Danish legal quality, NATO membership, and Western political alignment. South Africa offers mining sector maturity but faces operational challenges (energy, labor, infrastructure)
  • **First-mover risk vs established sector**: Skaergaard faces first-in-jurisdiction complexity, while Platreef benefits from South Africa's established mining infrastructure
  • **Strategic value**: Skaergaard's Western supply diversification value is higher than Platreef's, which adds supply to an already concentrated jurisdiction

    • US policy tailwinds are strengthening the investment case: a 132.83% anti-dumping duty on Russian unwrought palladium (Feb 2026), a Section 232 Presidential Proclamation on critical minerals (Jan 2026), and the creation of "Project Vault" — a $12 billion US Strategic Critical Minerals Reserve (Feb 2026).

    Development Timeline Comparison

  • **Platreef**: Shaft sinking underway, first production targeted 2026-2027. More advanced than Skaergaard
  • **Skaergaard**: Pre-feasibility, EIA not yet initiated. Production unlikely before 2032-2033
  • **Canadian juniors**: Most are 5-10+ years from production (if they advance at all)

    • Economic Comparison

  • **Cost position**: Skaergaard's estimated AISC of US$1,000-1,300/oz PdEq (after credits) is competitive with Platreef, despite the Arctic location
  • **Byproduct credits**: Skaergaard's gold credits are more significant than Platreef's nickel and copper credits at current prices (US$5,100/oz (Feb 2026) Au vs US$4.00/lb Ni)
  • **Capital efficiency**: Skaergaard's lower CapEx estimate (US$600M-1.25B vs US$1.8B) reflects smaller processing scale but also a less complex project

    • Risk Comparison

    Exploration License Risk: GRML holds three Mineral Exploration Licenses (MELs) totaling 877 km². MEL 2012-25 (Sødalen camp and airstrip, 16 km²) and MEL 2021-10 (754 km² exploration area) both expire December 31, 2026. MEL 2007-01 (107 km², hosts the Skaergaard Intrusion) remains active until December 31, 2027. These renewals represent a material near-term risk factor.

  • **Platreef risks**: South African operational environment (energy, labor, infrastructure), deep underground mining complexity, declining Bushveld grades
  • **Skaergaard risks**: Arctic construction and operations, greenfield development, regulatory first-mover complexity, financing for a first-in-jurisdiction project
  • **Shared risks**: Commodity price volatility, metallurgical performance, construction cost overruns

    • Valuation Implications

    PGM development companies are typically valued based on in-situ resource value, development stage, and jurisdiction quality:

  • **Platreef**: Market values the project at approximately US$5-10 per ounce of PdEq in-situ
  • **Skaergaard**: GRML's market valuation should be compared on a similar basis, with adjustments for gold content, jurisdiction quality, and development stage
  • **Jurisdiction premium**: Skaergaard's Western jurisdiction may justify a 20-30% valuation premium over equivalent South African projects
  • **Development stage discount**: Skaergaard's earlier development stage (pre-EIA) versus Platreef (construction) justifies a valuation discount

    • Key Differentiator

    The key differentiator for Skaergaard is the combination of:

  • Comparable resource scale to the largest competing projects
  • Significant gold byproduct that no other PGM developer offers
  • Western-aligned jurisdiction (Denmark/NATO) that provides strategic supply diversification value
  • Open-pit mining method (lower technical risk than underground)

    • This combination is unique among PGM development companies globally.

    Conclusion

    Skaergaard compares favorably with other PGM development projects on resource scale, jurisdiction quality, and multi-metal economics. The primary disadvantage is the earlier development stage compared to Platreef. For investors, GRML offers a unique combination of PGM exposure, gold diversification, and Western jurisdiction quality that is not available in any other single PGM development investment. The valuation should reflect this uniqueness through a strategic premium.
